3. Method: The WorldMind Framework

WorldMind establishes a training-free framework for bridging the gap between semantic planning and physical reality through autonomous Knowledgeable Experience Learning. Rather than relying on resource-intensive parameter updates, the system aligns the agent's internal world model by constructing a symbolic World Knowledge Repository (WKR) derived directly from environmental interaction. This mechanism synthesizes Process Experience via a Predict-Act-Verify loop to rectify physical hallucinations through prediction errors, while simultaneously distilling Goal Experience from successful trajectories to optimize procedural efficiency. By leveraging this accumulated knowledge for constrained simulation during inference, WorldMind ensures agent actions are both physically feasible and task-oriented without requiring gradient-based fine-tuning.
